Gallo Pinto

Description

Gallo Pinto, translates to "Painted Rooster", is the national dish of Nicaragua. It is said that Nicaragua runs on gallo pinto and it many people eat it every single day.
This dish is a great example of Nicaraguan food. Simple, unpretentious but no less delicious comfort food. Costa Ricans also make gallo pinto, and also claim
it as their national dish, however Nicaraguans invented it first, so we are cooking it Nicaragua style today! A couple of tips to make truly Nicaraguan gallo
pinto is to use day old rice, and also toast the rice before cooking them! It adds a carmelized flavour thats just so good!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of diced yellow onion
  • 1 cup of cooked white rice
  • 1 cup of cooked red beans
  • Pinch of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of olive or canola oil

Instructions

  1. Add 1 tablespoon of oil to a medium-high heat pan and saute diced onions until translucent.
  2. Add cooked red beans to the pan along with some of bean liquid and begin to cook beans until they begin to crisp or blister a bit. Add a pinch of garlic powder.
  3. Then add equal parts white rice and mix with beans.
  4. If needed, add more bean liquid to achieve darker color to the rice.
  5. For firm Gallo Pinto, cook for 10 minutes. For semi-wet Gallo Pinto, cook for 7 minutes
